Oracle Park

24 Willie Mays Plaza, San Francisco, CA 94107


Home to the San Francisco Giants, Oracle Park opened on April 11, 2000, and was conceived, built, and paid for by the late Giants owner Peter Magowan. Today, the park now boasts a nine-foot statue of famous ball-player Willie Mays, a stunning view of the San Francisco Bay, and a hangout area behind left field for kids of all ages. With a tickets that can fit your budget, remember to check out this modern battlefield and root for the home team!

Oakland-Alameda County Coliseum

7000 Coliseum Way, Oakland, CA 94621


Throughout its fifty-three year life, the Oakland-Alameda County Coliseumm - shortened to the Oakland Coliseum by locals - remains a multipurpose one. It remains a frequent host for music concerts of varying genres, but it is best known as the home of the Oakland Atheletics and the Oakland Raiders. However, whether the purpose is enjoy the spectacle of artists or witness a baseball game in historic seats, the visit is worth it.
